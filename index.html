E-commerce CRO UX Research

Rebuilding a premium e-commerce homepage around intent

Metzler GmbH

A research- and audit-driven redesign. I surfaced friction from usability tests, competitive analysis and an AI audit, prioritised the highest-impact fixes, and shipped a tokenised, dev-ready front end – a benefit-first hero, a consolidated trust strip, and the configurator finally given a clear entry point.

  • Usability testing
  • Competitive analysis
  • AI audit
  • Design system
  • Front-end handoff
View live ↗
The challenge

The live homepage left visitors to orient themselves instead of guiding them to the goal:

  • No value headline, and the configurator – the site’s highest-intent action – had no entry point.
  • No direct access to the FAQ.
  • Metzler’s advantages – the reasons to buy here over cheaper rivals – went unstated.
  • Trust signals were scattered: the nTV award showed only ceremony photos, with no link to the related products or an award info page.
  • The review section didn’t match the rest of the page visually.
  • 11 categories plus a 13-image carousel created heavy cognitive load.
What I did
  • Triangulated usability tests, competitive analysis and an AI audit to locate friction and rank it by impact.
  • Rewrote the hero benefit-first around the XDM10 (retrofit, no new cables) with two clear CTAs that point visitors to the goal.
  • Surfaced Metzler’s advantages and gave the FAQ a direct entry point, so the page answers “why Metzler”, not just “what”.
  • Consolidated scattered awards into one trust strip, quantified the “three reasons”, and linked the nTV award to related products and an award info page.
  • Aligned the review section with the overall design and gave the configurator a primary entry point – the site’s highest-margin intent, one click from load.
  • Delivered a tokenised, documented front end the dev team could build from directly.
Outcome
  • Cut time-to-comprehension from seconds of scrolling to a single glance.
  • Configurator and trust proof now sit where purchase intent forms.
  • Shipped as a reusable design system – tokens, components, handoff docs – not just a mockup.

The challenge

E-commerce UX Research AI

Turning a catalog page into a guided, intent-first experience

Metzler GmbH

A category-page redesign with a custom KI-Kaufberater (AI buying advisor): a 7-step needs-based quiz that translates what customers want into product specs, curates recommendations, and never leaves them at a dead end.

  • KI-Kaufberater
  • Filtering UX
  • FAQ
  • Empty states
  • UI consistency
View live ↗
The challenge

A standard catalog page asks customers to know the spec vocabulary – Zeitungsfach, Montageart, Fächer – before they can filter. High-intent buyers who can’t translate their needs into filters drop off, and choosing an unavailable combination dead-ends on a blank page.

What I did
  • Designed the KI-Kaufberater: a 7-step needs-based quiz (researched from the live catalog, not a mirror of the filters) that maps answers to product facets automatically.
  • Added graceful fallbacks: when nothing matches, it surfaces the closest alternatives instead of a blank page, and lets users email themselves the result or subscribe.
  • Surfaced Metzler’s advantages (why buy here over cheaper rivals) and added an FAQ section.
  • Tightened the layout: title to the top, sentence-case breadcrumbs, shorter sub-category labels, consistent hover across dropdown/strip/grid, cleaner filter borders and image frames.
  • Reworked the sub-category dropdown interface and featured the poster of the highlighted product inside it.
Outcome
  • The AI does the spec-translation, so customers shop by need, not by jargon.
  • No more dead ends – every filter path leads to products or a useful alternative.
  • A calmer, more consistent catalog with clearer hierarchy and reusable UI patterns.
Content design Trust IA

Turning a flat ‘About’ page into a trust-building hub

Metzler GmbH

A redesign of the Über uns page built around the different people who land on it – turning a flat company pitch into a page that informs, builds trust, and gives every visitor a clear next step.

  • Visitor journeys
  • Trust & awards
  • Founder’s note
  • Reviews
  • CTAs
View live ↗
The challenge

An About page is visited by very different people – the curious, those weighing trust, and those ready to get in touch – but the original spoke to none of them directly. It left visitors to orient themselves, with no clear path to learn more, build confidence, or act.

What I did
  • Designed the page around the three groups who land on an About page – the curious, the trust-seekers, and those ready to make contact – giving each a clear path.
  • For the curious: a founder’s note and technical info with a link to the production-process landing page.
  • For trust: trust and award badges linking to a dedicated section with more detail – and to the product the award belongs to – plus customer reviews.
  • For contact and action: a contact CTA, a home CTA, and newsletter sign-up.
Outcome
  • A page that meets each visitor’s intent – inform, reassure, or act.
  • Human proof and award context that build trust beyond price.
  • Every visitor leaves with a next step, and the page now feeds traffic into the catalog.
Product design Prototyping Handoff

A configurator that makes complex customization feel simple

Metzler GmbH

A guided configurator for custom mailbox & intercom sets – built from production constraints and conditional add-ons, validated with usability testing, and handed off to development.

  • User research
  • Prototyping
  • Usability testing
  • Conditional logic
  • Dev handoff
View live ↗
The challenge

Customising a mailbox/intercom set involves technical constraints and conditional add-ons that, left raw, overwhelm customers – and it’s easy to lose track of where you are in the process.

What I did
  • Gathered technical constraints and conditional add-on rules from production, and researched customer needs and preferences.
  • Designed and prototyped the guided configurator in constant alignment with developers, the product owner and production.
  • Ran usability testing and folded the findings – including keeping the whole step sequence visible so users never lose control – into the design.
  • Handed the validated flow to development with the documentation they needed to build it.
Outcome
  • A configurator that turns a potentially overwhelming purchase into a guided, confident decision.
  • Customers keep an overview and a sense of control throughout the process.
  • A validated, well-documented flow ready for development.
